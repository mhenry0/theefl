The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR):

This is the first level: A1 and A2.

A1 Level (Beginner)
Very basic language skills. Understands and uses familiar, everyday expressions and simple phrases. Can introduce themselves and others, and ask/answer basic personal questions. Engages in simple, slow, and clear conversations. Limited vocabulary and grammar, focusing on common words and phrases.

A2 Level (Elementary):
Slightly more developed language proficiency than A1. Understands sentences and common expressions on topics like personal information, family, and shopping. Engages in basic conversations, describes their background, and expresses immediate needs. Uses simple phrases and sentences to communicate in everyday situations. Begins to grasp basic grammar, including tenses, verb forms, and sentence structure.

This is the second level: B1 and B2.

B1 Level (Intermediate)
B1 represents an intermediate level of language proficiency. At this stage, individuals can understand and communicate in a second language with a moderate degree of fluency. They can handle everyday conversations and express their thoughts and opinions on familiar topics. B1-level speakers have a basic grasp of grammar and vocabulary, allowing them to interact in common social and practical situations.

B2 Level (Upper-Intermediate) B2 signifies an upper-intermediate level of language proficiency. At this level, individuals have a good command of the language, enabling them to engage in more complex conversations and activities. They can understand and discuss a wide range of topics, express opinions clearly, and use a broader vocabulary. B2-level speakers have an advanced understanding of grammar and can read and write with fluency, making them capable of more sophisticated communication and comprehension tasks.

This is the third level: C1 and C2.

C1 Level (Advanced): C1 represents an advanced level of language proficiency. At this stage, individuals have a high degree of fluency and can engage in complex, nuanced conversations on a wide range of topics. They can understand and express themselves effectively in both spoken and written language, demonstrating a rich vocabulary and a deep understanding of grammar. C1-level speakers and writers can handle challenging texts, participate in debates, and produce well-structured, detailed essays and reports. This level reflects a near-native or highly proficient command of the language.

C2 Level (Mastery or Proficiency):
C2 represents the highest level of language proficiency, often referred to as "Mastery" or "Proficiency." At this stage, individuals have near-native or native-level fluency in the language. They can effortlessly understand and communicate in a wide range of contexts, from everyday conversations to highly specialized or academic subjects. C2-level speakers have an extensive vocabulary, impeccable grammar, and exceptional language skills. They can read, write, speak, and listen at a level comparable to that of a native speaker, demonstrating a deep understanding of nuances and subtleties in the language. This level is typically achieved by experienced language learners or those who have lived in a foreign language environment for an extended period.
